SUBJECT A: Dream Chaser

Code Name:

SUB-DC127

Discovered by / at:

Bibi Ha, discovered October 19, 2024 at 1:27AM CST

Family:

There aren't many known organisms of SUB-DC127, and the closest related species so far has been determined to be ambition and desire.

Habitat:

The few organisms that have been studied were found in a location known as "Dreamscape".

Appearance:

The shape of its body appears to conjugate randomly in a spiral-like pattern, with its main parts being comprised of a bright green rectangular shape that fluctuates in width and height. It is very difficult to study up close, as any close physical proximity seems to trigger a defense mechanism where SUB-DC127 enters an spectral state. When it is in this state, it is difficult to see with the human eye and almost impossible to initiate physical contact. SUB-DC127 rarely sleeps but when it does, it enters its spectral state and ceases its trajectory. However, its body continues to conjugate itself.

Key Characteristics:

  • about 1.5 inches (3.8 cm) in diameter
  • its white spine can be seen when its main body parts are relatively small
  • seems to have an appetite, but doesn't eat anything
  • has a constant, consistent trajectory that doesn't seem to go anywhere

What we know so far about SUB-DC127: Originally thought to be independent creatures, recent studies have shown that species of SUB-DC127 form an attachment to people who attempt contact. Though it will not allow actual physical contact, SUB-DC127 will stay in the approximate area of the person of interest indefinitely (studies are still on-going). It does not to seem to be a sentient organism, but it does appear to respond to positive verbal affirmations concerning an individual's goals in life.
Guidelines for human interaction with SUB-DC127: There isn't much information as of now on human interaction. All that has been discovered from experiments and studies is that any attempt to grab or hold SUB-DC127 results in an almost complete invisible state. However, it appears to become agitated when 20,000 Hz of sound is released in its proximity (around 60 inch radius).
